Fresh Oyster Omelette served with extra cilantro.

Crisp on the edge, soft at the heart—an oyster omelette is where the sea meets the skillet in a sizzling love affair.

History

Origins of Oyster Omelette

The oyster omelette, also known as o-a-tsian (蚵仔煎) in Taiwanese Hokkien, is a beloved street food with roots in Fujian province, China. It’s thought to have originated among the Hokkien-speaking people, who later brought it to Taiwan, Southeast Asia, and beyond through migration.

In Taiwan, the dish became a staple in night markets, prized for its savory-sweet combo of fresh oysters, eggs, sweet potato starch, and a unique sweet chili sauce. Variations have popped up in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, and the Philippines, each adding its own twist—sometimes using mussels, more batter, or extra spice.

Today, oyster omelette is more than just a dish—it’s a nostalgic comfort food that tells a story of coastal culture, street food innovation, and the flavors of home.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • Fresh oysters
  • Eggs
  • Sweet potato starch
  • Water
  • Cilantro
  • Chili sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

Cooking Steps

  1. Mix sweet potato starch with water to form a batter.
  2. Heat oil in a skillet and pour in the batter.
  3. Add fresh oysters and cook until they start to curl.
  4. Crack eggs over the mixture and scramble gently.
  5. Cook until the omelette is set, then serve with cilantro and chili sauce.
